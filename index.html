SINHARAJA RAIN FOREST 

Located in south-west Sri Lanka, Sinharaja is the country's last viable area of primary tropical rainforest. More than 60% of the trees are endemic and many of them are considered rare. There is much endemic wildlife, especially birds, but the reserve is also home to over 50% of Sri Lanka's endemic species of mammals and butterflies, as well as many kinds of insects, reptiles and rare amphibians. the Insert panel.






Animals & Birds

The Sinharaja forest is a major eco-tourism destination in Sri Lanka, home to endemic wildlife such as the Red Faced Malkoha, Green Billed Coucal and Sri Lankan Blue Magpie. Preliminary studies have revealed a high degree of endemism among the butterflies, fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds and mammals.

Plants & Trees 

 Medicinal plants in rain forests are important bioresources in Sri Lanka, with species with special medicinal value threatened due to habitat destruction. Commonly used medicinal plants include Kohomba, Adatoda, Dorana, Binara, Duhudu, Murva, Kara, Kotalahi, Bandura, Iruraja, Wellangi, Wanaraja, Binkohoba, Iruraja, Binkohomba, Narilata.
